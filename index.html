Who We Are

Rescuing 30 Years of Neglect

Friends of Saron was formed in 2009 with the primary aim of rescuing the graveyard of the former Saron Chapel, Troedyrhiw, from almost three decades of neglect. The graveyard had become, in the words of local residents, a blot on the village.

"Names and dates carved into headstones have become a fixed part of the landscape — part of the identity of this place."

Troedyrhiw, meaning 'foot of the hill' in Welsh, sits in the valley of the River Taff, some 3 miles south of Merthyr Tydfil. Its growth from the early nineteenth century was linked to the locally abundant iron and coal reserves — and its people rest here.

In 2012, a Welsh Government funded programme under the Tidy Towns scheme carried out clearance work, followed by herbicide treatment supervised by Keep Wales Tidy. Our group continues the work of restoration, research, and community engagement.

Timeline

History of Saron Chapel & Graveyard

From its founding in the early nineteenth century to its ongoing restoration today, Saron has been a constant presence in the life of Troedyrhiw.

c. 1822
Saron Chapel Founded

Saron Chapel established in Troedyrhiw to serve the rapidly growing community of ironworkers and coal miners settling in the Taff Valley.

1893 – 1898
Notable Inscriptions

Among the most poignant memorials is the grave of William David and Maggie Mary, children of Rev. J.W. Price of Saron Villa — a reminder of the human stories contained within these grounds.

1951
Chapel Site Recorded

An official site plan of Saron Chapel and Graveyard produced, providing a valuable record of the layout of the grounds at mid-century.

c. 1980s
Period of Neglect Begins

Following the closure of Saron Chapel, the graveyard fell into a period of neglect spanning nearly three decades, becoming overgrown and inaccessible.

2009
Friends of Saron Formed

Community volunteers come together to form Friends of Saron, with the aim of restoring the graveyard and creating a Memorial and Wildlife Garden for the village.

2011
Saron Inspired Art Exhibition

A public art exhibition inspired by Saron brought community attention to the history and heritage of the site, raising awareness and support for the restoration project.

2012 – Present
Welsh Government Clearance & Ongoing Restoration

Funded clearance under the Tidy Towns scheme, followed by herbicide treatment from Keep Wales Tidy. Research, recording, and community activities continue to the present day.

Restoration Work
image-03-restoration.jpg
Headstone cleaning or repair work in progress

Graveyard Restoration

Our primary mission since 2009 has been the physical restoration of Saron Graveyard — clearing decades of overgrowth, stabilising headstones, and making the site accessible and safe for visitors.

Working in partnership with Welsh Government and Keep Wales Tidy, we have overseen funded clearance programmes, herbicide treatment, and ongoing maintenance by dedicated volunteers.

The ultimate goal is to transform the graveyard into a low-maintenance Memorial and Wildlife Garden — a lasting community asset for Troedyrhiw.

4
Research & Recording
image-04-research.jpg
Inscription recording, archival materials, or research activity

Historical Research

We research the history of Saron Chapel and the village of Troedyrhiw, creating as complete a burial record as possible in the absence of the original chapel records.

Each headstone inscription is carefully recorded, photographed, and documented. This work preserves stories that might otherwise be lost — from Victorian-era ministers to the families of ironworkers and miners.

Our research materials and educational packs have been shared with local schools, libraries, and heritage organisations across South Wales.

Community Events

Community Engagement

Friends of Saron is rooted in the local community. We produce and distribute regular newsletters (Saron News), organise public events, and have produced an Education Pack for use by local schools and youth groups.

We promoted and contributed to the Saron Inspired Art Exhibition in 2011, which brought the heritage of the site to a wider audience. We continue to promote and participate in events that connect people with their local history.

New volunteers are always welcome — no experience necessary, just a willingness to get involved.

6
Wildlife Garden Vision
image-06-wildlife.jpg
Wildflowers, butterflies, or natural planting at the site

Memorial & Wildlife Garden

Our long-term vision is to create a low-maintenance Memorial and Wildlife Garden on the Saron site — a place of quiet reflection that also supports local biodiversity.

Draft proposals have been developed for sympathetic planting, accessible pathways, and interpretation boards that tell the story of the site and its people.
